# Persona: Legitimacy-Fatigue Survivor

> "None of us are lying." (Claim: CLAIM-20260224-010)

## Primary Job-to-be-Done
Get taken seriously and reduce the effort of proving the problem is real while navigating long, uncertain diagnostic journeys. (Claims: CLAIM-20260224-010, CLAIM-20260224-001)

## Context of Use
Lives inside extended uncertainty with repeated consultations and conflicting opinions, compounded by access friction. (Claims: CLAIM-20260224-001, CLAIM-20260224-009)

## DO/SAY/WANT Snapshot
- **DO**: Stops using checkers/search for diagnoses to avoid more noise and reallocates effort to appointment preparation and clinician selection. (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-028)
- **SAY**: Defines belief as acknowledgement of impact even with normal tests, and defines dismissal as jokes or implying normal tests mean "fine". (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-029)
- **WANT**: Reduce retelling and receive a written, owned next-step plan with an escalation path that does not restart from zero. (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-031)

## Empathy Map Signals
- **Think**: "I have to perform..." and "I lost years of being doubted." (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-030)
- **Feel**: Anger/exhaustion and, over time, numbness/survival framing. (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-030)
- **Say**: "I am tired of proving I'm not lying. I need a plan." (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-030)
- **Do**: Prepares to compress story into limited visit time and avoids additional noisy tools. (Claims: CLAIM-20260225-028, CLAIM-20260225-031)

## Journey Map Notes
- **Breakdown**: "Come back if worse" without ownership or escalation path collapses momentum and increases emotional cost. (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-031)
- **30% easier**: Clinician-ready structure plus explicit ownership and routing reduces the need to perform credibility repeatedly. (Claims: CLAIM-20260225-031, CLAIM-20260225-030)

## Representative Interview Excerpts (Variants)
- **Variant A**: "I stopped using checkers because I don't want more noise... I can't waste minutes." (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-028)
- **Variant B**: "Breakdown is when a doctor says 'we did everything' and I still have symptoms." (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-031)

## Top 3 Motivations and Pain Points (SAY/DO)
- **Motivation/Pain 1**: Exhausted by stigma and the repeated need to defend legitimacy; feels forced into credibility performance. (Claims: CLAIM-20260224-010, CLAIM-20260225-030)
- **Motivation/Pain 2**: Progress stalls with appointment logistics, normal test results, and affordability stressors. (Claim: CLAIM-20260224-009)
- **Motivation/Pain 3**: Wants something clinicians can use to accelerate recognition and reduce delay. (Claim: CLAIM-20260224-014)

## Ranked Decision Criteria (SAY/VALUE)
1. **Legitimacy support**: Language and structure that preserves credibility and avoids "normal tests" dismissal cues. (Claims: CLAIM-20260224-010, CLAIM-20260225-029)
2. **Access friction reduction**: Practical support through logistics and constraints. (Claim: CLAIM-20260224-009)
3. **Clinician-ready artifacts**: Reusable clinician-ready stories and written plans that reduce retelling and shorten the diagnostic odyssey. (Claims: CLAIM-20260224-014, CLAIM-20260225-031)

## Barriers, Anxieties, and Triggers (DO)
- **Barriers**: Conflicting opinions and repeated inconclusive investigations. (Claim: CLAIM-20260224-001)
- **Anxieties**: "Normal" results plus ongoing symptoms undermine confidence and trust. (Claim: CLAIM-20260224-009)
- **Triggers**: Persistence without a clear name pushes continuous escalation attempts. (Claim: CLAIM-20260224-001)

## Preferred Channels and Touchpoints (DO)
- Clinician encounters: repeated visits during diagnostic uncertainty. (Claim: CLAIM-20260224-001)
- Services navigation: friction points (stigma/logistics/affordability). (Claim: CLAIM-20260224-009)

## Design Implications
- **Messaging**: Explicitly acknowledge legitimacy and stigma; avoid dismissal cues. (Claim: CLAIM-20260224-010)
- **Offer**: Provide clinician-usable structured outputs to reduce delay and repetition. (Claim: CLAIM-20260224-014)
- **UX/Service**: Reduce repeated retelling burden by structuring history for reuse and providing a written next-step plan with an escalation path. (Claims: CLAIM-20260225-031, CLAIM-20260224-001)
- **Trust**: Make constraints visible; align expectations with uncertainty reality. (Claim: CLAIM-20260224-001)
- **Channel**: Fit inside fragmented systems and access constraints. (Claim: CLAIM-20260224-009)
