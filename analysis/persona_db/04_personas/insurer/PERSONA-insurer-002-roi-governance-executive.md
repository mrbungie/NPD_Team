# Persona: ROI Governance Executive

> "If we can't justify it in HTA terms and measure outcomes compliantly, we can't adopt it." (Claims: CLAIM-20260225-013, CLAIM-20260225-015)

## Primary Job-to-be-Done
Invest in technology that is defensible to auditors/citizens and can be justified as a policy/procurement decision with measurable outcomes under privacy-compliant data governance. (Claims: CLAIM-20260225-013, CLAIM-20260225-015, CLAIM-20260225-036)

## Context of Use
Balances investment appetite with procurement justification needs; expects HTA-ready evidence, outcome measurement, and interoperability fit with national backbone constraints. (Claims: CLAIM-20260225-013, CLAIM-20260225-015, CLAIM-20260225-014)

## DO/SAY/WANT Snapshot
- **DO**: Treats monitoring credibility and outcome-ownership commitments as gating steps that frequently stall procurement. (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-038)
- **SAY**: Frames adoption as public accountability: measurable, explainable decisions for auditors and citizens. (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-036)
- **WANT**: Outcome measures and governance defined upfront; political and clinical legitimacy risks actively managed. (Claims: CLAIM-20260225-037, CLAIM-20260225-039)

## Empathy Map Signals
- **Think**: If it looks like experimenting on citizens without governance, it will be vetoed. (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-039)
- **Feel**: Confidence with a clean decision memo and monitoring plan; anxiety when the story is vendor-led and vague. (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-036)
- **Say**: Rejects "we'll figure it out" governance and demands upfront definition. (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-037)
- **Do**: Uses appraisal/HTA framing and monitoring plans to reduce decision risk. (Claims: CLAIM-20260225-013, CLAIM-20260225-036)

## Procurement Journey Notes
- **Breakdown**: When outcome measurement and data access ownership are not assignable, proposals become unapprovable. (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-038)
- **30% easier**: Provide a minimal evidence pack aligned to HTA expectations plus a monitoring plan and reporting approach that avoids manual work. (Claims: CLAIM-20260225-036, CLAIM-20260225-038)

## Representative Interview Excerpts (Variants)
- **Variant A**: "We need defensible adoption... explainable to auditors and citizens." (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-036)
- **Variant B**: "We stall when the monitoring plan isn't credible and outcomes aren't defined." (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-038)

## Top 3 Motivations and Pain Points (SAY/DO)
- **Motivation/Pain 1**: Needs procurement decisions supported by appraisal and recommendation artifacts that are defensible externally. (Claims: CLAIM-20260225-013, CLAIM-20260225-036)
- **Motivation/Pain 2**: Needs outcome measures and governance roles defined upfront; vague outcomes or "we'll figure it out" governance is a rejection trigger. (Claims: CLAIM-20260225-015, CLAIM-20260225-037)
- **Motivation/Pain 3**: Needs interoperability fit, plus credible monitoring/ownership commitments so adoption does not stall after initial enthusiasm. (Claims: CLAIM-20260225-014, CLAIM-20260225-038)

## Ranked Decision Criteria (SAY/VALUE)
1. **HTA-ready evidence**: Appraisal-grade artifacts to support procurement recommendations. (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-013)
2. **Measurement + privacy**: Outcome measurement criteria and privacy-compliant data use expectations. (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-015)
3. **Interoperability fit**: Alignment with EDS/FSE and national system constraints. (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-014)

## Barriers, Anxieties, and Triggers (DO)
- **Barriers**: Adoption stalls when outcome ownership, data access, and monitoring plans are not commit-able or would create manual reporting burden. (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-038)
- **Anxieties**: Non-compliant data use or unclear governance creates procurement risk. (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-015)
- **Triggers**: Procurement decisions require appraisal-grade evidence readiness. (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-013)

## Preferred Channels and Touchpoints (DO)
- Procurement processes that rely on appraisal and recommendation artifacts. (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-013)

## Design Implications
- **Messaging**: Frame adoption in HTA-ready evidence terms and measurable outcomes under compliant data governance. (Claims: CLAIM-20260225-013, CLAIM-20260225-015)
- **Offer**: Provide outcome measurement design and privacy compliance artifacts. (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-015)
- **UX/Service**: Make interoperability constraints and integration approach explicit (EDS/FSE interaction). (Claim: CLAIM-20260225-014)
