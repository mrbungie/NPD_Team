# Claim: Reassurance-seeking behavior and sensitivity to dismissal

## Statement
Anxious patients repeatedly seek reassurance signals (refreshing results until they see "not dangerous") and feel invalidated when clinicians dismiss fear or fail to acknowledge it.

## Evidence Basis
- "I refresh results until I find the one that tells me it's not dangerous." (Source: SOURCE-20260225-020)
- "Believed is when they don't laugh at my fear." (Source: SOURCE-20260225-019)

## Reasoning
This captures DO/SAY patterns that affect trust: validation and calm explanation reduce compulsive reassurance checking.
