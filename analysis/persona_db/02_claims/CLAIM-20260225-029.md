# Claim: "Normal tests" can be used as a dismissal cue; belief is acknowledgement of impact

## Statement
Patients define being believed as acknowledgement of suffering and life impact even when tests are normal, and define not-being-believed as dismissal, jokes, or implying that "normal tests" mean they are fine.

## Evidence Basis
- "Believed is when they don't make jokes and they acknowledge the suffering. Not believed is when they say 'normal tests' and imply I'm fine." (Source: SOURCE-20260225-021)
- "Believed is when they acknowledge the impact on my life even if tests are normal." (Source: SOURCE-20260225-022)

## Reasoning
This is a do/say/want signal that should drive language, clinician-facing outputs, and expectation-setting.
