# Claim: Relief-in-community vs dread-in-clinic emotional split

## Statement
Patients experience a split empathy pattern: relief in online communities but dread/shame around appointments; they fear disappearing without a matching story and often feel embarrassed after visits.

## Evidence Basis
- "If I don't find someone like me, I feel like I'm disappearing." (Source: SOURCE-20260225-017)
- "I feel relief in the community and dread in the waiting room. After appointments I often feel embarrassed because I didn't explain it well." (Source: SOURCE-20260225-017)
- "Mostly shame and fear. Relief when someone online says 'me too'." (Source: SOURCE-20260225-018)

## Reasoning
These empathy signals point to product work around preparation, language, and feedback loops that reduce shame and improve confidence.
