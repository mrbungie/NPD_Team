# Claim: Communities function as emotional support and routing intelligence

## Statement
Community-dependent patients use peer stories to feel less alone and to match symptom clusters to practical routing clues (which specialist to see, which tests to ask about).

## Evidence Basis
- "I read other people's stories to feel less alone. Then I check if someone had the same symptom cluster and what specialist they saw." (Source: SOURCE-20260225-017)

## Reasoning
This is both an empathy signal (loneliness) and a product requirement (make peer knowledge actionable and clinician-transferable).
