# Claim: Political and clinical hidden vetoes block adoption

## Statement
Hidden vetoes in insurer adoption are often political and clinical: proposals can be blocked if they appear to lack governance legitimacy, if clinicians feel solutions are imposed without evidence, or if pathway impact is not measurable.

## Evidence Basis
- "Hidden veto is political: if it looks like we are experimenting on citizens without governance, it stops." (Source: SOURCE-20260225-025)
- "Hidden veto: if clinicians feel it's imposed without evidence, they reject it." (Source: SOURCE-20260225-026)
- "The decision is: does it change the pathway in a measurable way without creating governance problems?" (Source: SOURCE-20260225-026)

## Reasoning
This turns "buying" into a multi-actor legitimacy process; success requires explicit governance and clinician-facing evidence.
