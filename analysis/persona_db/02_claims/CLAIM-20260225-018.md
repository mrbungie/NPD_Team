# Claim: Dismissal fear creates credibility-performance and shame/anger cycles

## Statement
Patients rehearse and "perform" credibility because they fear dismissal; they oscillate between urgency and exhaustion and feel shame/anger when minimized.

## Evidence Basis
- "I'm scared that if I don't present it perfectly, I'll be dismissed again. I rehearse how to say it so it sounds credible." (Source: SOURCE-20260225-015)
- "Before: nervous and focused. During: I go blank if I feel rushed. After: either relief (if they give a plan) or anger and shame (if they minimize it)." (Source: SOURCE-20260225-015)
- "I keep thinking: what if this is serious and I'm wasting time? But also: what if they think I'm exaggerating?" (Source: SOURCE-20260225-016)

## Reasoning
These are empathy-map signals that predict adoption and drop-off, and highlight why tone, structure, and clinician-facing outputs matter.
