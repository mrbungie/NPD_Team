# Claim: Plans are anxiety regulators; no-plan moments cause panic spirals

## Statement
Lack of a concrete plan (especially after leaving a visit) creates "empty space" that patients fill with panic; a clear plan temporarily reduces anxiety and helps maintain momentum.

## Evidence Basis
- "Fear first. Then exhaustion. If I get a concrete plan, I relax for a few days." (Source: SOURCE-20260225-019)
- "The worst is the empty space after I leave the clinic. No plan means I fill it with panic." (Source: SOURCE-20260225-020)
- "Breakdown is when the tool makes me panic and I don't know who to ask safely." (Source: SOURCE-20260225-020)

## Reasoning
This ties the empathy map (fear/exhaustion) to a clear product lever: structured planning and safe escalation pathways.
