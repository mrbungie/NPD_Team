# Claim: Prolonged diagnostic uncertainty includes repeated consultations and "no name"

## Statement
Prolonged diagnostic uncertainty often involves repeated consultations, inconclusive investigations, conflicting medical opinions, and lack of a recognizable name for the condition.

## Evidence Basis
- Parents report the pre-diagnosis period as protracted and distressing, marked by repeated consultations, inconclusive investigations, conflicting opinions, and "absence of a recognisable name". (Source: SOURCE-20260224-001)

## Reasoning
This is direct evidence of the behavioral pattern and emotional context of a diagnostic odyssey. Transfer to adult unknown-symptom contexts should be treated as Medium confidence due to the pediatric/parent context.
